My grad school state­ments

It’s around that time of year again when peo­ple are be­gin­ning to the grad school ap­pli­ca­tion process. I found it very use­ful to read the state­ments of pre­vi­ous ap­pli­cants when I was writ­ing my state­ments, so I’m mak­ing my own ap­pli­ca­tion ma­te­ri­als avail­able as a re­source for fu­ture ap­pli­cants.

All schools re­quire a state­ment of pur­pose, which ex­plains your spe­cific re­search in­ter­ests. Some also re­quire a per­sonal state­ment, which is a bit less de­fined / less con­sis­tent in de­f­i­n­i­tion across schools, but gen­er­ally pro­vides an op­por­tu­nity to speak to your per­sonal back­ground and mo­ti­va­tions for grad­u­ate school.
